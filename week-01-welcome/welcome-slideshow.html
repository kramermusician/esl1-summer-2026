ESL1 / Summer

Welcome to a class about how English really gets in.

It is not about being perfect. (Nobody is.) It is about your body, your ears, and the city outside this room.

02
Idea 1

The body learns faster than the brain.

My dad taught me to juggle in third grade. My hands learned the rhythm before my brain did. Words are the same. Move while you say them. The word stays.

dodge hop swipe chop snap clap tap point wave nod shake stomp flick reach lean wiggle recoil pivot gesticulate contort
03
Idea 2

You forget the flashcard.

You do not forget the soup.

I learned my first Portuguese in restaurants in Massachusetts. I held the plate. I asked the cook for the word. The word came home with me.

04
Idea 3

You already know more English than you think.

piano forte jazz rhythm guitar microphone solo album tempo studio concert melody

Music has been speaking English to you for years. (You did not even notice.) We start there.

05
Idea 4

Mistakes are not failures.

They are how the brain learns.

When you drop the ball, you learn where your hand should be. When you say the wrong word, your mouth learns the right one. Drop. Try again. That is most of the class, honestly.

06

07
Idea 6

Boston is part of our classroom.

We will walk on Mass Ave. We will ride the T. We will listen to people order coffee. The city teaches you things this room cannot.

08
What we will do together
  • Write a little, every class.
  • Walk and listen, every week.
  • Juggle, sometimes. (Yes, really.)
  • Talk about your songs, your food, your home.
  • Be wrong out loud, with friends.
09
A promise

By August, you will surprise yourself.

I have watched it happen every summer for years. It will happen to you too. (Trust me on this one.)
